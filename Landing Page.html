Breitling's History

In 1915,Breitling launched a wristwatch featuring a chronograph complication.Gaston Breitling,

Leon's son,had the inspired idea to introduce a push-piece at 2o'clock to start,stop and reset the

chronograph functions.

In 1923,the start and stop functions were separated from the reset.The crown incorporated the reset

function allowing the wearer to time successive events by repeatedly pressing the start/stop button

without having to reset the registers.

The evolution of the chronograph continued.Willy Breitling,Leon's grandson,had the idea to add a

second push piece at 4 o'clock for resetting the stopwatch function.This design was subsequently

copied by many watch brands and remains the norm for modern-day chronographs.
